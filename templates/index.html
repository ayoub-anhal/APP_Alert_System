ABOUT FIRE DETECTION

The dry season in Morocco, lasting from late spring until the onset of the rains, is a critical time when the risk of fires increases across various regions, including urban areas, bus routes, and industrial zones. A small spark can quickly escalate into a dangerous fire, threatening homes, buildings, transportation networks, and livelihoods. These fires not only pose risks to human life and property but also damage essential infrastructure, disrupt local economies, and endanger wildlife habitats. Fire Detection is here to help monitor and control these risks. The system detects even the smallest trace amounts of fire gases and raises an early alarm before the flames spread, giving firefighters valuable time to respond quickly and prevent extensive damage to buildings, roads, and communities.

LOSSES DUE TO FIRES IN MOROCCO

Fires in Morocco pose significant problems across several sectors. Each year, they destroy approximately 3,000 hectares of forests, leading to biodiversity loss and a reduction in natural resources. In the agricultural sector, around 10% of the affected lands suffer considerable damage, impacting crops and livestock. Infrastructure, such as roads and rural housing, is regularly damaged, requiring costly repairs. Lastly, the local economy is severely impacted, with rural populations, often dependent on natural resources, experiencing significant income losses due to these fires.
